Prehab Guardian

Smart Knee Movement Monitor

Built a wearable system that tracks knee movement in real-time and alerts athletes when they're moving in ways that could hurt them. Uses two tiny sensors and machine learning to understand dangerous movement patterns.

TinyML STM32 IMU Sensors Signal Processing Machine Learning Embedded C Bluetooth LE Biomechanics

What I Built

A smart wearable that watches how athletes move and warns them when they're about to hurt their knee.

The Problem

ACL tears happen in about 50 milliseconds when athletes land weird or cut too hard. There's no way to warn someone that fast with current tech. Most "solutions" just tell you to be careful or wear a bulky brace.

What I Made

Two tiny sensors that stick to your leg like tape. They talk to each other and figure out how your knee is moving 200 times per second. When the system sees a dangerous pattern (knee going inward, landing too straight, etc.), it buzzes to warn you instantly.

Why It's Cool

It's like having a coach watching your every move, but the coach can see things happen in slow motion and react faster than humanly possible. Plus it's basically invisible - just feels like wearing regular athletic tape.

How It Works

Built this to track knee movement with two small sensors and warn athletes instantly when they're moving dangerously. Everything happens on the device - no cloud, no delays.

  • Two motion sensors (one on thigh, one on shin)
  • Fast ARM processor that can think in real-time
  • Smart filtering to ignore noise and focus on knee movement
  • Tiny AI model that learned what dangerous movements look like
  • Bluetooth to alert coaches and log data
  • All built into flexible circuits thinner than tape

How I Built It

The technical challenges and how I solved them.

🔧

The Hardware

Had to fit a powerful computer, two sensors, wireless radio, and battery into something thinner than a band-aid. Used cutting-edge flexible circuits and picked components for speed over everything else.

STM32H743 (480MHz beast)
Dual LSM6DSM sensors
nRF52840 for Bluetooth
Flexible PCB design
🧮

Making Sense of Motion

Raw sensor data is noisy and confusing. Built smart filters to figure out exactly how the knee is moving in 3D space, 200 times per second, without getting fooled by vibrations or artifacts.

Kalman filtering magic
200Hz data processing
Sub-5ms computation
3D orientation tracking
🤖

Teaching It to Think

Trained a neural network to recognize dangerous patterns, then squeezed it down to fit on a tiny chip. Had to get creative with optimization to make it both smart and fast.

Neural network (32→16→8→3)
8-bit number crunching
Fits in 100KB
Thinks in milliseconds
📡

Talking Wirelessly

When something bad is about to happen, everyone needs to know immediately. Built custom Bluetooth protocols to get alerts out in under 50 milliseconds.

Bluetooth LE 5.0
<50ms alert time
10+ meter range
Custom data protocols
⚡

Making It Last

Athletes need this to work all day without thinking about charging. Implemented aggressive power saving - the CPU sleeps between measurements and everything shuts down when not needed.

Smart power management
CPU speed scaling
Sleep between samples
All-day battery life

Making It Invisible

The hardest part: making high-tech electronics that athletes actually want to wear. Researched flexible circuit manufacturing and figured out how to embed everything in regular athletic tape.
